## üìù EXECUTIVE SUMMARY TEMPLATE

**Use for:** All grant proposals  
**Length:** 200-300 words  
**Purpose:** Standalone summary of entire proposal

```
Title: [PROJECT TITLE]

This proposal seeks [FUNDING AMOUNT] over [DURATION] to [PRIMARY OBJECTIVE IN ONE SENTENCE].

PROBLEM/GAP:
[Current analytical methods/approaches have limitation X, which prevents/limits Y, causing problem Z for stakeholders/field.]

APPROACH:
We will develop [NOVEL METHOD/TECHNOLOGY] that addresses this gap through [KEY INNOVATION]. Our approach leverages [YOUR UNIQUE EXPERTISE/RESOURCES] to achieve [SPECIFIC OUTCOMES].

SIGNIFICANCE:
Success will enable [SCIENTIFIC ADVANCE], provide [PRACTICAL BENEFIT], and train [NUMBER] graduate students in [RELEVANT SKILLS]. Expected outputs include [2-3 PUBLICATIONS], [METHODS/TOOLS DEVELOPED], and [ADDITIONAL IMPACTS].

TEAM:
Principal Investigator Dr. Nazmul Alam brings [RELEVANT EXPERTISE], supported by collaborators [NAME/INSTITUTION] providing [COMPLEMENTARY EXPERTISE]. 

FEASIBILITY:
Preliminary results demonstrate [KEY FINDING], and we have access to [CRITICAL RESOURCES/FACILITIES]. The project timeline and budget are realistic and justified.

This research aligns with [FUNDING AGENCY]'s priorities in [RELEVANT PRIORITY AREA] and Malaysia's national focus on [RELEVANT NATIONAL PRIORITY].
```

## üî¨ BACKGROUND & SIGNIFICANCE TEMPLATES

### **Template 1: Technical Problem Statement**

```
CURRENT STATE:
[Method/technique X] is widely used for [application], with [number] publications annually and applications in [industries/fields]. However, current approaches face three critical limitations:

1. [LIMITATION 1]: [Specific problem] results in [negative consequence], affecting [stakeholders]. For example, [specific example or data].

2. [LIMITATION 2]: [Specific problem] leads to [negative outcome]. Current workarounds involve [existing solutions], but these are inadequate because [reasons].

3. [LIMITATION 3]: [Specific problem] prevents [desired application/outcome]. This gap particularly impacts [specific area/group].

OPPORTUNITY:
Recent advances in [related field/technology] and our preliminary work on [your contribution] suggest that [your approach] could overcome these limitations by [mechanism/innovation].
```

### **Template 2: Green Chemistry Justification**

```
ENVIRONMENTAL & ECONOMIC IMPERATIVE:
Traditional [analytical method/process] consumes [X liters] of [harmful solvent] per [time period], generating [waste amount] and costing [$ amount] annually for a typical [lab/facility]. Industry-wide, this translates to [large-scale impact].

Regulatory pressure is mounting: [cite relevant regulations, e.g., EU REACH, EPA guidelines]. Moreover, [X companies/institutions] have committed to [sustainability goals] by [date].

Our preliminary work at [Deciem/previous institution] demonstrated that [green alternative] can reduce [solvent use by 90%/other metric], saving [$50K annually/other benefit] while maintaining [performance metric]. Scaling this approach to [target application] addresses both environmental responsibility and economic efficiency.
```
