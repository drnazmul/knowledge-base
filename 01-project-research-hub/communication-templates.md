## üí° EMAIL BEST PRACTICES

### **Subject Lines:**
‚úÖ Clear and specific: "Research Collaboration Discussion - NADES-SPME Project"
‚úÖ Include action if needed: "Action Needed: Review by Jan 15"
‚ùå Avoid: Vague subjects like "Quick question" or no subject

### **Opening:**
‚úÖ Appropriate greeting: "Dear Prof." for formal, "Hi Dr." for established relationship
‚úÖ Check spelling of names!
‚ùå Avoid: Too casual if relationship not established

### **Body:**
‚úÖ Get to point quickly (busy people appreciate brevity)
‚úÖ Use bullet points for multiple items
‚úÖ Bold or underline key action items
‚úÖ Specific dates/times (not "next week")
‚ùå Avoid: Long paragraphs, burying the ask

### **Closing:**
‚úÖ Clear next steps or call to action
‚úÖ Professional sign-off
‚úÖ Complete contact information
‚ùå Avoid: Multiple exclamation points, emoji (unless established casual relationship)

### **Timing:**
‚úÖ Send during business hours when possible
‚úÖ Allow reasonable time for responses (2-3 business days)
‚úÖ Follow up politely if no response after 1 week
‚ùå Avoid: Sending late night or weekends for non-urgent matters

### **Attachments:**
‚úÖ Mention attachments in email body
‚úÖ Use clear filenames: "Alam_NADES-Project_Results_Jan2025.pdf"
‚úÖ Check file size (compress if > 10MB)
‚ùå Avoid: Unclear names like "Draft.doc" or "Data.xlsx"
