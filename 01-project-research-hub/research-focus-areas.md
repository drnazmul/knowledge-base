## ðŸ”¬ RESEARCH AREA 1: NADES-BASED SPME INNOVATION

### **Core Concept**
Natural Deep Eutectic Solvents (NADES) as novel extraction phases for Solid Phase Microextraction (SPME), combining green chemistry principles with cutting-edge analytical performance.

### **Why It Matters**
- Traditional SPME coatings rely on synthetic polymers (environmental concerns)
- NADES are biodegradable, non-toxic, tunable, and often cheaper
- Aligns with global push for green analytical chemistry
- Opens new applications previously impossible with traditional coatings

---

### **Specific Research Questions**

**1. NADES Synthesis & Characterization**
- Which NADES formulations are suitable for SPME coating?
- How do hydrogen bonding networks affect extraction selectivity?
- What's the thermal stability range for different NADES?
- Can we predict extraction behavior from NADES structure?

**2. NADES-SPME Coating Development**
- How to immobilize NADES on fiber substrates?
- What coating thickness gives optimal performance?
- How does coating stability compare to traditional phases?
- Can we create task-specific coatings for target applications?

**3. Extraction Performance**
- What's the extraction efficiency vs. traditional coatings?
- How do NADES-SPME perform with different analyte classes?
- What are the optimal extraction conditions?
- How does matrix complexity affect performance?

**4. Method Validation**
- Precision, accuracy, LOD/LOQ for NADES-SPME methods
- Robustness testing (aging, storage, repeated use)
- Matrix effects in complex samples
- Regulatory compliance (ICH Q2) for pharmaceutical applications

## ðŸ”¬ RESEARCH AREA 2: SINGLE-CELL METABOLOMICS

### **Core Concept**
Miniaturized SPME approaches for metabolomic profiling at single-cell level, enabling unprecedented insight into cellular heterogeneity and function.

### **Why It Matters**
- Traditional methods analyze cell populations (hide individual variability)
- Single-cell analysis reveals heterogeneity critical for understanding disease
- SPME's miniaturization capability ideal for microvolume sampling
- Opens new frontiers in biomedical research and diagnostics

---

### **Specific Research Questions**

**1. Miniaturization Challenges**
- What's the minimum SPME fiber size for single-cell sampling?
- How to prevent cell lysis during extraction?
- What sensitivity is achievable with microvolume samples?
- Can we quantify metabolites from single cells?

**2. Method Development**
- Optimal extraction conditions for cellular metabolites
- Fiber coating selection for diverse metabolite classes
- Sample preparation procedures
- Temporal resolution (how fast can we sample?)

**3. Validation & Applications**
- Reproducibility across individual cells
- Comparison with traditional cell population methods
- Application to disease-relevant cell models
- Integration with imaging techniques
