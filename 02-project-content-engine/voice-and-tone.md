## üìè WRITING GUIDELINES

### **Sentence Structure**

**Do:**
- Mix short and long sentences for rhythm
- Start with strong verbs
- Use active voice primarily
- Vary sentence beginnings

**Example:**
```
"I optimized this method at Deciem. The results surprised me. Traditional 
approaches required 5 hours of prep time. Our SPME method? Fifteen minutes."
```

**Don't:**
- All short sentences (choppy)
- All long sentences (exhausting)
- Passive voice overuse
- Repetitive structure

---

### **Paragraph Structure**

**Do:**
- 2-4 sentences per paragraph (for digital)
- One main idea per paragraph
- Use line breaks generously
- Topic sentence first (usually)

**Don't:**
- Walls of text (7+ sentences)
- Multiple ideas crammed together
- No breathing room
- Bury the lead

---

### **Word Choice**

**Prefer:**
- Strong, specific verbs: "discovered" over "found out"
- Concrete nouns: "40% improvement" over "significant increase"
- Active constructions: "I developed" over "it was developed"
- Simple where possible: "use" over "utilize"

**Avoid:**
- Weak verbs: "make," "do," "get"
- Vague adjectives: "very," "really," "quite"
- Unnecessary jargon
- Clich√©s: "game-changer," "think outside the box"

---

### **Technical Terms**

**When to Use:**
- Essential to meaning: "SPME," "LC-MS/MS"
- Standard in field: "chromatography," "extraction"
- No simple alternative: "hematocrit," "partition coefficient"

**When to Explain:**
- First mention in content
- Addressing students or broad audience
- Complex or specialized terms
- Acronyms (always spell out first)

**Example:**
```
"We used Solid Phase Microextraction (SPME) - essentially a tiny fiber 
coated with material that selectively absorbs target compounds. Think of 
it like a molecular sponge."
```

---

### **Numbers & Data**

**Do:**
- Be specific: "47% improvement" not "significant improvement"
- Use numbers < 10 spelled out in prose, numerals for data
- Include context: "reduced from 5 hours to 15 minutes (95% faster)"
- Visualize when helpful

**Don't:**
- Overload with statistics
- Use data without interpretation
- Claim precision you don't have
- Forget units

---

### **Examples & Analogies**

**Use Them Frequently:**
- Makes abstract concepts concrete
- Increases relatability
- Improves retention
- Shows real-world relevance

**Good Analogies:**
```
"SPME is like dipping a sponge in water - it absorbs what's dissolved 
and carries it to your instrument for measurement."

"Matrix effects in bioanalysis are like trying to hear someone at a 
concert - the signal (your analyte) is there, but background noise 
(other compounds) makes it hard to detect."
```

**Use Analogies From:**
- Everyday life
- Other sciences
- Common experiences
- Visual/physical processes
