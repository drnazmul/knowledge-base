## 2026 LINKEDIN ALGORITHM ‚Äî KEY PRINCIPLES

### The 2026 Reality (Richard van der Blom Algorithm Insights Report):
- Organic views down 50%
- Engagement down 25%
- Follower growth down 59%
- **BUT**: Creators who adapted to the new rules are performing better than ever
- The algorithm now rewards: expertise, authenticity, meaningful conversation
- The algorithm now punishes: clickbait, engagement bait, generic AI content, external links

### The Three Core Signals:
1. **Dwell Time** (#1 ranking factor): How long users spend reading/viewing your content. Longer = better. Posts holding attention 30+ seconds get broader distribution.
2. **Engagement Velocity (Golden Hour)**: First 60-90 minutes determine ~70% of your post's total reach. Early meaningful comments are critical.
3. **Niche Authority / Topic Expertise**: LinkedIn tracks your posting patterns to determine your expertise areas. Consistent posting on 1-2 topics trains the algorithm to show your content to the right audience.

### Content Format Performance (2026 ranking):
1. **PDF Carousels (Document posts)**: Highest dwell time. Swipes = extended engagement. 303% more engagement than single images. Sweet spot: 8-12 slides.
2. **Native Video**: +69% performance boost. Short-form (60-90 seconds) vertical video. Must have captions. Hook in first 3 seconds.
3. **Text Posts with Personal Photo**: Strong for personal branding. 1,200-2,000 characters optimal. "See more" cutoff at 210 characters ‚Äî first 2 lines must hook.
4. **Polls**: Easy engagement, good for audience research. Use sparingly (1-2x/month).
5. **External links**: Deprioritized by ~60%. If sharing links, provide substantial native value in the post itself first.

### Post Structure Best Practices:
- **Hook (first 2 lines)**: Must stop the scroll. Use: surprising stat, contrarian take, personal story opener, specific number, or direct question.
- **Body**: Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max). Line breaks and white space for mobile readability. One idea per section.
- **Closing**: End with a question or conversation prompt (NOT "comment YES if you agree" ‚Äî that's penalized as engagement bait).
- **Optimal length**: Text posts: 1,200-2,000 characters. Carousels: 8-12 slides.
- **Hashtags**: 3-5 relevant ones. Too many hurts performance.
- **Tagging**: Max 5 people, only those likely to respond.
- **No emojis overload**: A few strategic emojis OK, but don't look like a bot.

### Posting Schedule:
- **Frequency**: 3-5 posts per week for optimal algorithm training
- **Best times**: Tuesday 8-10 AM, Thursday after 6 PM (but test for your audience). Generally: weekday mornings 8-9 AM or afternoons 2-3 PM in target timezone.
- **Post reactivation**: Comment on or reshare your own post after 8 or 24 hours to push it back into feeds.
- **Consistency > virality**: Regular high-quality posts beat occasional brilliant ones.

### Content Mix Ratio (2026 Framework):
- **60% Educational**: Frameworks, practical tips, how-tos, metrics explanations, industry insights. This builds trust and expertise signal.
- **20% Personal/Story**: Career lessons, research journeys, behind-the-scenes, honest reflections. This builds connection and generates highest engagement.
- **10% Thought Leadership**: Industry opinions, trend commentary, contrarian takes (backed by reasoning), predictions. This generates discussion/comments.
- **10% Promotional**: Announcements, publications, achievements, YouTube video links. Keep this small.

### What Gets PENALIZED in 2026:

- ‚ùå Generic AI-generated content (algorithm detects it)
