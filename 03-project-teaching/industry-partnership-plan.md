### **2. INDUSTRY-SPONSORED PROJECTS** ‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Concept:** Companies propose real problems; students solve them (Major Projects)

**Process:**

**1. Identify Industry Partners:**
- Reach out to my network
- Present value proposition (free R&D, student training)
- Establish MOU with willing companies

**2. Project Scoping:**
- Company describes problem/need
- I work with them to scope appropriate for Master's students
- Define deliverables and timeline
- Agree on IP and confidentiality terms

**3. Student Engagement:**
- Students choose from industry-proposed projects
- Work throughout semester
- Regular updates to company sponsor
- Professional-quality final deliverable

**4. Outcomes:**
- Student: Real project experience, networking, potential job
- Company: Solution to real problem, talent pipeline
- University: Industry reputation, potential funding

**Example Projects:**

**Pharmaceutical Company:**
- "Develop green analytical method for [drug] in plasma"
- "Reduce solvent use in current QC procedure"
- "Evaluate NADES for API extraction"

**Cosmetics Company:**
- "Green extraction method for Malaysian medicinal plant"
- "Sustainable preservative system evaluation"
- "Life cycle assessment of natural vs. synthetic ingredient"

**Environmental Consulting:**
- "Cost-effective green method for pesticide monitoring"
- "Validate portable analytical device for field use"
- "Green chemistry alternatives for industrial client"

**My Experience:**
At Deciem, I solved similar problems:
- 90% solvent reduction (real, not theoretical)
- $50K annual savings (quantified)
- Regulatory compliant (practical requirement)

Students working on these see REAL impact.

**Target Sites:**
- Pharmaceutical manufacturing
- Cosmetics production facility
- Environmental testing lab
- Chemical plant with green processes
- Biorefinery or bio-based production

**Logistics:**
- 1-2 site visits per semester

**Before Visit:**

**After Visit:**

- Discuss improvements possible

**My Role:**
