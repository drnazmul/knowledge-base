## üèõÔ∏è ABOUT MQA

### **What is MQA?**

**Malaysian Qualifications Agency (Agensi Kelayakan Malaysia)**
- Statutory body under Ministry of Higher Education (MOHE)
- Established under Malaysian Qualifications Agency Act 2007
- Responsible for quality assurance in Malaysian higher education

**Website:** www.mqa.gov.my

**Mission:** To ensure quality and recognition of higher education qualifications in Malaysia

---

### **MQA Functions**

1. **Accreditation:** Approve and accredit programs and qualifications
2. **Quality Assurance:** Monitor and ensure education quality standards
3. **Malaysian Qualifications Register (MQR):** Maintain register of accredited qualifications
4. **Malaysian Qualifications Framework (MQF):** Implement and maintain national framework
5. **Recognition:** Evaluate and recognize foreign qualifications

---

## üìã MALAYSIAN QUALIFICATIONS FRAMEWORK (MQF)

### **Overview**

The MQF is a unified national system that:
- Describes all qualifications in Malaysia
- Provides consistent standards across institutions
- Facilitates credit transfer and recognition
- Aligns with international frameworks

---

### **MQF Levels**

| Level | Qualification | Typical Duration | Key Characteristics |
|-------|--------------|------------------|-------------------|
| 1-3 | Certificate | Varies | Vocational skills |
| 4 | Diploma | 2-3 years | Technical knowledge |
| 5 | Advanced Diploma | 1-2 years | Specialized skills |
| 6 | Bachelor's | 3-4 years | Disciplinary knowledge |
| 7 | **Master's** | **1-2 years** | **Advanced knowledge** |
| 8 | PhD | 3-5 years | Original research |

**Green & Sustainable Chemistry = Level 7 (Master's)**

---

### **Level 7 (Master's) Descriptors**

**Knowledge & Understanding:**
- Advanced knowledge of field acquired systematically
- Critical understanding of theories, principles, concepts
- Knowledge of current research and developments
- Specialized knowledge in chosen area

**Cognitive Skills:**
- Critical analysis, evaluation, synthesis of complex ideas
- Research design and problem-solving in complex situations
- Independent thinking and judgment
- Originality in application of knowledge

**Practical Skills:**
- Mastery of specialized techniques/methods
- Apply advanced skills in professional contexts
- Research skills for investigation
- Independent learning capability

**Interpersonal & Communication:**
- Communicate complex ideas effectively (written and oral)
- Work independently and collaboratively
- Leadership and ethical professional practice

**Professional, Ethics & Sustainability:**
- Ethical awareness and professional responsibility
- Demonstrate responsibility for own learning
- Autonomous practice in complex situations
- Commitment to sustainability (relevant for our course!)

## üìÑ COURSE ACCREDITATION PROCESS

### **Overview**

**For NEW Course (like Green & Sustainable Chemistry):**

Universities must submit course for MQA approval BEFORE offering it to students. This is called **Provisional Accreditation (PA)**.

**Timeline:** 3-6 months from submission to decision

**Process:**

```
1. Internal University Approval
   ‚Üì
2. Prepare MQA Documentation Package
   ‚Üì
3. Submit to MQA Online System (MyMOHES)
   ‚Üì
4. MQA Document Review (2-3 months)
   ‚Üì
5. MQA Site Visit (if required)
   ‚Üì
6. MQA Panel Decision
   ‚Üì
7. Provisional Accreditation Granted (2-5 years)
   ‚Üì
8. Offer Course to Students
   ‚Üì
9. First Cohort Graduates
   ‚Üì
10. Apply for Full Accreditation (FA)
    ‚Üì
11. MQA Review & Site Visit
    ‚Üì
12. Full Accreditation Granted (5 years, renewable)
```

---

### **Required Documentation**

**Main Documents:**

1. **Program Overview**
   - Background and rationale
   - Objectives and learning outcomes
   - Career prospects for graduates
   - Demand analysis (industry needs, enrollment projections)

2. **Program Structure**
   - Total credit hours (typically 40-45 for Master's)
   - Core courses, electives, thesis/project
   - Course sequencing and prerequisites
   - Delivery mode and schedule

3. **Course Information Forms (CIF)** - For EACH course
   - Course title, code, credit value
   - Course Learning Outcomes (CLO)
   - Mapping to Program Learning Outcomes (PLO)
   - Content outline (week-by-week syllabus)
   - Teaching and learning methods
   - Assessment strategy (types, weights, CLO alignment)
   - Resources (textbooks, references)

4. **Program Learning Outcomes (PLO)**
   - Must align with MQF Level 7 descriptors
   - Typically 8-12 PLOs covering:
     - Knowledge & understanding
     - Cognitive skills  
     - Practical skills
     - Interpersonal skills
     - Ethics & professionalism

5. **PLO-CLO Mapping Matrix**
   - Shows how each course contributes to PLOs
   - Demonstrates program coherence

6. **Assessment Strategy**
   - Assessment types and rationale
   - Alignment with learning outcomes
   - Grading rubrics
   - Moderation procedures

7. **Academic Staff**
   - Qualifications (CV for each)
   - Staff-student ratio
   - Teaching load
   - Research and professional development

8. **Resources & Facilities**
   - Library resources (books, journals, databases)
   - Laboratories and equipment
   - ICT facilities
   - Student support services

9. **Quality Assurance**
   - Mechanisms for monitoring program quality
   - Student feedback systems
   - Continuous improvement processes
   - External examiner procedures

10. **Industry Involvement**
    - Advisory panels
    - Industry partnerships
    - Internship/practicum arrangements
    - Graduate employability tracking

## üìä LEARNING OUTCOMES FRAMEWORK

### **Writing Learning Outcomes**

**Format:** Students will be able to [Action Verb] + [Object] + [Context/Condition]

**Action Verbs by Level (Bloom's Taxonomy):**

**Remember (Lower Order):**
- List, define, recall, identify, recognize

**Understand (Lower Order):**
- Explain, describe, summarize, interpret, classify

**Apply (Higher Order):**
- Use, demonstrate, apply, implement, solve

**Analyze (Higher Order):**
- Analyze, compare, contrast, differentiate, examine

**Evaluate (Higher Order):**
- Evaluate, justify, critique, assess, judge

**Create (Higher Order):**
- Design, construct, develop, formulate, create

**For Master's Level:** Emphasis on HIGHER ORDER (Apply, Analyze, Evaluate, Create) = 60-70% of outcomes

---

### **Example PLOs for Green Chemistry Master's**

**PLO1 - Knowledge:**
Demonstrate comprehensive knowledge of green chemistry principles, sustainable chemical practices, and their applications in industrial contexts.

**PLO2 - Cognitive Skills:**
Analyze complex chemical processes critically and design sustainable alternatives using systematic problem-solving approaches and quantitative assessment methods.

**PLO3 - Practical Skills:**
Apply advanced analytical and synthetic techniques for green chemistry applications, including modern instrumentation and computational tools.

**PLO4 - Communication:**
Communicate scientific information effectively to diverse audiences through written reports, oral presentations, and professional discussions.

**PLO5 - Teamwork:**
Collaborate effectively in multidisciplinary teams to address complex sustainability challenges in chemical sciences.

**PLO6 - Leadership & Autonomy:**
Demonstrate independent thinking, self-directed learning, and leadership in promoting sustainable practices in professional settings.

**PLO7 - Ethics & Professionalism:**
Apply ethical principles and professional standards in chemical practice with commitment to environmental stewardship and social responsibility.

**PLO8 - Lifelong Learning:**
Engage in continuous professional development and adaptation to emerging trends in green chemistry and sustainability.

---

### **Example CLOs for Green Chemistry Course**

**CLO1 (Knowledge - PLO1):**
Explain the 12 Principles of Green Chemistry and their applications across chemical industries.

**CLO2 (Application - PLO2, PLO3):**
Evaluate chemical processes using green chemistry metrics and recommend sustainable alternatives based on quantitative analysis.

**CLO3 (Synthesis - PLO2, PLO3, PLO7):**
Design green chemical processes or methods for specific applications, justifying choices based on environmental, economic, and safety considerations.

**CLO4 (Communication - PLO4):**
Communicate green chemistry concepts and analyses effectively to technical and non-technical audiences.

## üìà ASSESSMENT REQUIREMENTS

### **MQA Assessment Principles**

**Must Include:**

1. **Variety of Methods**
   - Examinations
   - Assignments/reports
   - Presentations
   - Projects
   - Laboratory work
   - Continuous assessment

2. **Alignment with Outcomes**
   - Each CLO must be assessed
   - Assessment methods appropriate for outcome level
   - Clear mapping: Assessment ‚Üí CLO ‚Üí PLO

3. **Balance**
   - Formative (for learning) and summative (for grading)
   - Continuous assessment + final exam
   - Individual and group work

4. **Transparency**
   - Assessment criteria clear to students
   - Rubrics provided
   - Grading standards explicit

5. **Quality Assurance**
   - Moderation of assessments
   - External examiner review (for Master's/PhD)
   - Analysis of student performance
   - Continuous improvement

---

### **Typical Assessment Distribution for Master's Course**

**Recommended:**
- Continuous Assessment: 60-70%
- Final Examination: 30-40%

**For Green Chemistry Course:**
- Quizzes: 10%
- Laboratory: 15%
- Case Study: 20%
- Project: 25%
- Final Exam: 30%
- **Total: 100%**

**Pass Mark:** Typically 50% overall, with no component < 40%

**1. Don't Copy-Paste**
- Don't just copy from other universities
- Tailor to your context and students
- Show distinctive features

**2. Don't Be Vague**
- Avoid generic learning outcomes
- Don't say "understand" without showing how assessed

- Ensure resources available
- Be realistic about what's achievable
- Don't claim expertise you don't have

- Allow time for thorough documentation
- Get feedback from colleagues
- Quality over speed

## üöÄ SITE VISIT PREPARATION

### **What Happens in MQA Site Visit**

**When:** After document review, typically for new programs or major reviews

**Duration:** 1-2 days

**Panel:** 3-5 external experts (academics, industry)

**Activities:**
- Facility inspection (classrooms, labs, library)
- Staff interviews (program coordinator, instructors)
- Student interviews (if program already running)
- Document review (physical evidence)
- Presentation by program team

---

### **What Panel Looks For**

**Resources:**
- Adequate laboratories and equipment
- Sufficient library resources
- Qualified academic staff
- Student support services

**Quality:**
- Clear quality assurance mechanisms
- Student feedback systems
- Continuous improvement evidence
- External examiner involvement

**Delivery:**
- Teaching and learning approaches
- Assessment methods and moderation
- Student workload realistic
- Industry connections evident

**Outcomes:**
- Graduate employability
- Program alignment with needs
- Student achievement data
- Stakeholder satisfaction

---

### **Preparation Tips**

**Documentation:**
- Have all documents organized and accessible
- Prepare extra copies for panel
- Evidence of quality (student work samples, feedback reports)

**Facilities:**
- Clean, organized, well-maintained
- Equipment functional
- Safety protocols visible

**People:**
- Brief staff on program details
- Students prepared for interviews (if applicable)
- Industry partners available (if possible)

**Presentation:**
- Professional program overview (15-20 min)
- Highlight distinctive features
- Show alignment with MQA requirements
- Demonstrate quality and commitment
