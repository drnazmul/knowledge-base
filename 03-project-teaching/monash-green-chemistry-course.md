## üéì COURSE RATIONALE

### **Why This Course Matters**

**Global Context:**
The chemical industry faces unprecedented pressure to adopt sustainable practices. Traditional chemical processes generate significant waste, consume excessive energy, and rely on hazardous materials. Green chemistry offers systematic approaches to minimize environmental impact while maintaining economic viability.

**Malaysian Context:**
Malaysia's chemical industry contributes significantly to GDP but faces challenges:
- Environmental regulations tightening (DOE requirements)
- Industry demand for sustainability expertise
- National commitment to SDGs and Paris Agreement
- Opportunity to lead ASEAN in green chemistry

**Student Value:**
Graduates gain:
- Competitive advantage in job market (sustainability roles growing 20%+ annually)
- Skills applicable across industries (pharma, cosmetics, manufacturing, environmental)
- Foundation for research careers in green chemistry
- Understanding of regulatory compliance and implementation

**Program Fit:**
Complements Monash Malaysia's:
- Sustainability commitment and strategic priorities
- Research strengths in chemistry and environmental science
- Industry partnerships and engagement focus
- Innovation in teaching and learning

## üìö COURSE CONTENT

### **12-Week Structure**

**WEEK 1: Introduction to Green Chemistry**

**Learning Objectives:**
- Define green chemistry and sustainability in chemical context
- Explain the historical development and drivers of green chemistry
- Describe the 12 Principles of Green Chemistry with examples

**Topics:**
- What is green chemistry? (vs. environmental chemistry)
- Historical context: From Love Canal to present
- Economic and regulatory drivers
- The 12 Principles: Overview and interconnections
- Success stories: Industrial implementations

**Pre-Class (Flipped):**
- Watch: "Green Chemistry Introduction" video (15 min)
- Read: Anastas & Warner (1998) seminal paper
- Quiz: Basic concepts (formative)

**In-Class Activities:**
- Discussion: Personal experiences with chemical waste/hazards
- Group work: Identify green vs. non-green in everyday products
- Case study: Ibuprofen synthesis (traditional vs. green route)

**Assessment:** Participation, formative quiz

**Industry Connection:** Pharmaceutical industry case studies

---

**WEEK 2: Principle 1-3 - Waste Prevention, Atom Economy, Safer Synthesis**

**Learning Objectives:**
- Calculate atom economy and E-factor for chemical reactions
- Identify waste sources in chemical processes
- Design reactions with improved atom economy

**Topics:**
- Principle 1: Waste prevention strategies
- Principle 2: Atom economy (calculation and optimization)
- Principle 3: Less hazardous chemical syntheses
- Protecting groups: Problem and alternatives
- Catalysis for waste reduction

**Pre-Class:**
- Read: Chapter on waste metrics
- Complete: Online atom economy calculations
- Watch: Industry case study video

**In-Class:**
- Workshop: Calculate E-factor for various processes
- Problem-solving: Redesign synthesis routes
- Discussion: Trade-offs between yield and atom economy

**Laboratory (Week 2-3):**
Experiment 1: Comparing traditional vs. green synthesis (e.g., esterification)
- Measure atom economy, waste, time, cost
- Compare safety profiles

**Assessment:** Lab report (formative feedback)

---

**WEEK 3: Principle 4-6 - Safe Chemicals, Solvents, Energy Efficiency**

**Learning Objectives:**
- Assess chemical hazards using safety data
- Select safer alternatives using systematic approaches
- Evaluate solvent alternatives including NADES

**Topics:**
- Principle 4: Designing safer chemicals (structure-activity relationships)
- Principle 5: Safer solvents and auxiliaries
- Solvent selection guides and alternatives
- **Natural Deep Eutectic Solvents (NADES)** - [My research expertise!]
- Principle 6: Energy efficiency in chemical processes
- Room temperature reactions, catalysis

**Pre-Class:**
- Read: Solvent selection guide (ACS GCI)
- Watch: NADES introduction video
- Activity: Analyze SDS for common solvents

**In-Class:**
- Workshop: Solvent selection for specific applications
- Demonstration: NADES synthesis and properties
- Group analysis: Energy consumption in chemical plants

**Guest Lecture:** Industry speaker on solvent reduction initiatives

**Research Integration:** My work on NADES-based SPME coatings

---

**WEEK 4: Principle 7-9 - Renewable Feedstocks, Derivatives, Catalysis**

**Learning Objectives:**
- Compare renewable vs. petrochemical feedstocks
- Understand biomass conversion challenges and opportunities
- Explain catalytic approaches for green synthesis

**Topics:**
- Principle 7: Use of renewable feedstocks
- Biomass chemistry and biorefinery concepts
- Principle 8: Reduce derivatives (protecting groups)
- Principle 9: Catalysis (homo/heterogeneous, bio)
- Malaysian context: Palm oil, natural rubber, medicinal plants

**Pre-Class:**
- Read: Biorefinery overview
- Case study: Bio-based chemicals market analysis
- Video: Catalysis fundamentals review

**In-Class:**
- Debate: Food vs. fuel vs. chemicals
- Workshop: Design process using Malaysian biomass
- Problem sets: Catalytic cycle analysis

**Malaysian Context:** Local renewable resources and opportunities

---

**WEEK 5: Principle 10-12 - Degradation, Real-time Analysis, Accident Prevention**

**Learning Objectives:**
- Design molecules for degradation
- Understand Process Analytical Technology (PAT)
- Apply inherent safety principles

**Topics:**
- Principle 10: Design for degradation
- Biodegradability and persistence
- Principle 11: Real-time analysis for pollution prevention

### **Teaching Strategies**

**1. Problem-Based Learning**
Real industrial problems as learning contexts:
- Students work in groups to solve authentic challenges
- Requires research, analysis, collaboration
- Presents solutions to class
- Examples: "Reduce waste in pharmaceutical synthesis," "Design green analytical method for cosmetics QC"

**2. Case-Based Learning**
Analysis of real industrial implementations:
- Success stories and failures
- Cost-benefit analysis
- Decision-making under constraints
- Examples: Pfizer's green chemistry, Deciem's solvent reduction

**3. Inquiry-Based Laboratory**
Not cookbook labs - guided inquiry:
- Students design experiments (with boundaries)
- Troubleshoot problems
- Analyze results critically
- Compare approaches

**4. Industry Integration**
Real-world connection throughout:
- Guest lectures from industry
- Industry-proposed projects
- Site visits (if feasible)
- Case studies from local companies

**5. Research-Led Teaching**
My active research informs teaching:
- Latest developments in NADES, SPME
- Research methods and thinking
- Real data and results
- Connection to frontier research

## üî¨ LABORATORY COMPONENT

### **Laboratory Philosophy**

**Not Traditional "Cookbook" Labs:**
- Inquiry-based approach
- Students make decisions
- Compare green vs. traditional
- Quantitative analysis emphasized
- Safety and sustainability integrated

**Learning Objectives:**
- Practical skills in green techniques
- Experimental design and troubleshooting
- Data analysis and interpretation
- Report writing
- Safety and environmental awareness

---

### **Laboratory Experiments (8 sessions)**

**Session 1-2: Green vs. Traditional Synthesis**
- Week 2-3
- Compare esterification: traditional acid catalyst vs. enzymatic
- Measure atom economy, E-factor, time, cost, safety
- Analysis: GC or HPLC

**Session 3-4: SPME Method Development**
- Week 6-7  
- Develop SPME method for analyte extraction
- Compare to liquid-liquid extraction (LLE)
- Optimize conditions (fiber type, time, temperature)
- Quantitative analysis: LC-MS or GC-MS

**Session 5-6: Natural Product Extraction with NADES**
- Week 9-10
- Synthesize NADES from natural components
- Extract bioactives from Malaysian medicinal plant
- Compare to traditional organic solvent extraction
- Quantify extraction efficiency

**Session 7-8: Green Chemistry Project (Major Project Labs)**
- Week 11-12
- Students work on major project experimental component
- Supervised independent work
- Data collection for project reports

---

### **Laboratory Safety & Sustainability**

**Safety:**
- Pre-lab safety briefings
- SDS review for all chemicals
- PPE requirements enforced
- Emergency procedures clear
- Green chemistry reduces hazards but safety still paramount

**Sustainability:**
- Minimize chemical use (microscale where possible)
- Proper waste segregation and disposal
- Solvent recovery when feasible
- Energy-efficient equipment
- Calculate environmental impact of labs
