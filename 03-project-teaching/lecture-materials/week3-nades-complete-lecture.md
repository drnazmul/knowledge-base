## **SEGMENT 2: NADES Design Challenge (30 min)**

### **Objectives:**
- Apply knowledge of NADES composition
- Practice molecular reasoning
- Develop design thinking
- Collaborate in teams

### **Activity: Design a NADES**

**My Setup:**
"You work for a pharmaceutical company extracting a bioactive compound from plants. Current method uses methanol‚Äîexpensive, toxic, generates waste. Your job: Design a NADES alternative."

**The Challenge:**
"Your target molecule is moderately polar, has hydroxyl groups, molecular weight ~250. You need to extract from dried plant material at room temperature."

**Group Task (20 min):**

**Each group gets:**
- Envelope with component cards:
  - HBA options: Choline chloride, betaine, proline
  - HBD options: Glucose, fructose, citric acid, urea, glycerol
  - Property reference sheet
  - Calculation worksheet

**Instructions:**
1. Choose HBA and HBD combination
2. Determine molar ratio (1:1, 1:2, 2:1, etc.)
3. Calculate mass needed to make 100g NADES
4. Predict properties (polarity, viscosity, stability)
5. Justify your design (Why these components? Why this ratio?)

**Worksheet Guides Them:**
- Consider: Polarity matching with target
- Consider: Viscosity for practical use
- Consider: Cost and availability
- Consider: Safety and environmental profile
- Consider: Stability (won't crystallize)

**My Role During Group Work:**
- Circulate to all groups
- Ask probing questions (not give answers):
  - "Why did you choose glucose over citric acid?"
  - "How will that ratio affect viscosity?"
  - "What's your reasoning for 2:1 instead of 1:2?"
- Provide hints if groups are stuck
- Ensure all group members participating

**Common Issues to Address:**
- Groups choosing incompatible components ‚Üí "Check the hydrogen bonding capability..."
- Not considering practical properties ‚Üí "Think about actually using this in a lab‚Äîwhat would be challenging?"
- Forgetting about cost ‚Üí "This is industry‚Äîeconomics matter!"

---

**Group Presentations (10 min total):**

**Format:**
Each group has 90 seconds at their table to present (I rotate, others listen):
- "Our NADES: [Components] at [ratio]"
- "We chose this because..."
- "Predicted properties:"
- "Potential challenges:"

**I Take Notes:** Record designs on board for comparison

**Class Observations (3 min):**
"Looking at all designs, what patterns do you notice?"
- Most chose choline chloride (why? It's versatile and well-studied)
- Ratios varied (what's the impact?)
- Trade-offs evident (low viscosity vs. high polarity)

**Key Teaching Moment:**
"Notice there's NO single 'correct' answer. Real engineering involves trade-offs. Your job is to justify choices with evidence and reasoning."

## **SEGMENT 3: Live NADES Synthesis Demonstration (20 min)**

### **Objectives:**
- See actual NADES preparation
- Observe physical properties
- Discuss practical considerations
- Connect theory to practice

### **My Demonstration Setup:**

**Safety First:**
"Even though NADES are safer than organic solvents, we still follow proper protocols..."
[PPE, fume hood, proper labeling]

### **Synthesis:**

**Recipe:** Choline chloride : Glucose (1:2 molar ratio)

**Process (I narrate while doing):**

**Step 1: Weighing (5 min)**
"I'm weighing 13.96g choline chloride and 36.04g glucose for 50g total NADES. Let's verify this ratio is correct..."
[Show calculation on document camera]

**Students help:** "Can someone calculate the moles of each component? Does my 1:2 ratio check out?"
[Student does calculation on board]

**Step 2: Mixing (5 min)**
"I'm adding both to beaker and stirring. Watch what happens..."
[Heat gently to 80¬∞C with stirring]

**Running Commentary:**
- "Notice it's not dissolving immediately‚Äîthese are solid components forming a liquid mixture. The eutectic phenomenon is fascinating..."
- "Temperature helps, but we could do this at room temperature (just takes longer)"
- "See how viscous it's becoming? That's a NADES characteristic we need to consider for applications"

**Step 3: Cooling & Testing (5 min)**
"Now we cool to room temperature and test properties..."

**Tests (students observe):**
- pH test: "Approximately neutral‚Äîimportant for stability"
- Viscosity demonstration: "More viscous than water, less than honey‚Äîaffects pumping, stirring"
- Stability: "We'll leave this at room temperature for weeks‚Äîit won't crystallize"

**Step 4: Comparison (5 min)**
"Let's compare to methanol (our original solvent)..."

**Side-by-Side:**
| Property | Methanol | Our NADES |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Toxicity | High (LD50: 5628 mg/kg) | Low (components are food-grade) |
| Vapor Pressure | High (evaporates readily) | Negligible (no VOC emissions) |
| Flammability | High (flashpoint 11¬∞C) | Non-flammable |
| Cost | ~$50/L | ~$5/L (components) |
| Biodegradability | Moderate | Excellent |
| Viscosity | Low (0.59 cP) | High (~200 cP) |

**Discussion:**
"What do you observe?"
- [Students note]: NADES wins on safety, environmental, cost
- [Students note]: But loses on viscosity (practical challenge)

"This is REAL green chemistry‚Äînot perfect, but significantly better with manageable trade-offs."

## **SEGMENT 5: Industry Application Case Study (20 min)**

### **Objectives:**
- Apply NADES knowledge to real industrial problem
- Develop cost-benefit analysis skills
- Practice stakeholder communication
- Understand implementation barriers

### **Case Study: Cosmetics Industry**

**My Introduction:**
"Now let's apply what we've learned. I'll share a real scenario from my time at Deciem (The Ordinary)..."

### **The Scenario:**

**Background:**
- Natural cosmetics company, emphasis on clean ingredients
- Quality control lab analyzes products daily (active ingredients, contaminants, stability)
- Current extraction method: Ethanol-based (for natural product analysis)
- Problem: High solvent costs, waste disposal issues, safety concerns

**The Data:**
- Lab uses 200L ethanol/month
- Cost: $50/L = $10,000/month on solvent alone
- Disposal: Additional $3,000/month
- Safety incidents: 2 minor spills/year
- Regulatory: Stricter VOC regulations coming

**The Request:**
Management asks: "Can we switch to something greener? What are the options and trade-offs?"

### **Group Activity (15 min):**

**Task:**
Your group is the R&D team. Prepare recommendation for management:

1. **Technical Assessment:**
   - Can NADES replace ethanol for this application?
   - Which NADES would you test?
   - What are technical risks?

2. **Economic Analysis:**
   - Cost comparison (NADES vs. ethanol)
   - Implementation costs (equipment, training, validation)
   - Payback period

3. **Implementation Plan:**
   - Phase 1: What to test first?
   - Timeline: How fast can you transition?
   - Risk mitigation: What could go wrong?

4. **Management Pitch:**
   - One slide summary (you'll present to "management")
   - Focus: ROI, risk, timeline

**Materials Provided:**
- Cost data for NADES components
- Equipment requirements
- Regulatory information
- Template for business case

**My Role:**
- Act as technical advisor when groups ask questions
- Push them to quantify (not just say "it's greener"‚ÄîHOW MUCH greener?)
- Ensure they consider implementation realities

### **Presentations (5 min total):**

**Format:** One group presents to class as "management"
[I role-play as CEO, asking tough questions]

**Sample Dialogue:**

*Group:* "We recommend switching to choline chloride-glycerol NADES..."

*Me (as CEO):* "How much will this cost upfront? When do we break even?"

*Group:* "Initial investment is $30K for... we break even in 18 months..."

*Me:* "What if it doesn't work? What's our fallback?"

*Group:* "We propose pilot study first with just one product line..."

*Me:* "Okay, I like the conservative approach. What's the timeline?"

**Teaching Moment (After Role-Play):**

"Notice what happened: Technical feasibility alone isn't enough. You need business case, risk assessment, implementation plan. This is real-world green chemistry‚Äîit's as much about communication and business as it is about chemistry."
