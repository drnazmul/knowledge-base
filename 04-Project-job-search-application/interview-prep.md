## üìã COMMON INTERVIEW QUESTIONS

### **CATEGORY 1: Research**

**"Tell us about your research."**

**Your Answer Framework:**
- 2-minute elevator pitch (big picture)
- "My research advances green analytical chemistry through three areas: NADES-SPME development, single-cell metabolomics, and industrial applications..."
- Connect to their department ("I see opportunities to collaborate with [faculty name] on...")
- End with question ("What research areas is the department prioritizing?")

**"How will you fund your research?"**

**Your Answer:**
- "Multi-pronged approach: Malaysian grants (FRGS Year 1, MRUN Year 2), industry contracts (leveraging my Deciem/Dalriada network), international collaborations"
- Specific: "I'm preparing FRGS proposal on NADES-Malaysian medicinal plants for submission in [month]"
- Show realism: "I understand startup grants from university typically cover [X], and I'd need access to LC-MS..."

**"How will students be involved in your research?"**

**Your Answer:**
- "Master's students: Defined projects within larger program (NADES synthesis, method optimization)"
- "PhD students: Independent research extending my work"
- "Undergrads: Summer research, honors projects"
- "Publications with students prioritized - I published 6 papers during my PhD showing commitment to student authorship"

### **CATEGORY 2: Teaching**

**"Describe your teaching philosophy."**

**Your Answer (Be Authentic, Not Generic):**
- "Research-led, active learning, industry relevance"
- **Concrete example:** "In my Green Chemistry course, Week 3 on NADES - I share actual data from my USM collaboration, including challenges we're facing, inviting students to propose solutions..."
- "I use flipped classroom - pre-class videos, in-class application - maximizes face-to-face time for deep learning"

**"How would you handle a struggling student?"**

**Your Answer:**
- "First, diagnose the issue - is it understanding, study skills, external factors?"
- "Specific example from UWaterloo: Student failing quizzes despite attending - discovered test anxiety. Worked with student services, provided practice tests, result: passed with B"
- "Office hours, peer study groups, university resources, accommodations when needed"
- "My job is student success, not just content delivery"

**"What courses can you teach?"**

**Your Answer (Be Specific):**
- "Prepared to teach: Green Chemistry (complete course designed!), Analytical Chemistry, Instrumental Analysis, SPME Methods, Bioanalytical Chemistry, all lab courses"
- "Can develop new courses as needed - took 2 weeks to design complete Green Chemistry course showing ability to build curriculum quickly"

### **CATEGORY 3: Fit & Vision**

**"Why our university?"**

**Your Answer (Research Them!):**
- **Monash example:** "Three reasons: (1) New Green Chemistry Master's - I've designed the complete course, ready to implement, (2) Research culture values industry partnerships - my network provides immediate connections, (3) Existing collaboration with Prof. Goh shows I'm already invested in Sunway/Malaysian research community"
- Never: "It's a job" or "Location" as primary reason

**"Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"**

**Your Answer:**
- "Leading researcher in green analytical chemistry, established lab with 3-4 graduate students, externally funded (FRGS/MRUN/industry), published 10-15 additional papers, developed industry advisory panel for program, potentially promoted to Associate Professor"
- "Contributing to department through curriculum development, student mentoring, service"
- Shows ambition but commitment to THIS university

**"How will you contribute beyond research and teaching?"**

**Your Answer:**
- "Service: Willing to serve on departmental committees, contribute to MQA accreditation, mentor junior faculty"
- "Outreach: Public lectures, high school visits, science fairs"
- "Industry: Build partnerships benefiting students and university reputation"
- "Community: Engage Malaysian chemistry community, conference organizing"

### **CATEGORY 4: Challenges & Scenarios**

**"What's your biggest weakness as a teacher?"**

**Your Answer (Honest But Not Disqualifying):**
- "Early in teaching, I tried to cover too much content, sacrificing depth for breadth. Now I focus on fewer topics done deeply, trusting students can learn details independently if foundation is strong"
- Always follow with: "Here's how I addressed it..."

**"How would you handle research disagreement with collaborator?"**

**Your Answer:**
- "Respectful scientific discussion based on data"
- "Example: During my PhD, disagreed with interpretation - presented alternative analysis, we published both perspectives, reviewers appreciated thoroughness"
- "Collaboration thrives on diverse perspectives, not groupthink"

**"How do you balance research and teaching?"**

**Your Answer:**
- "Integration, not balance - my research enriches teaching, teaching generates research questions"
- "Time management: Protected research time (mornings), accessible teaching time (afternoons/office hours)"
- "Realistic: Some semesters research-heavy (grant deadlines), others teaching-heavy (new course prep)"

## üé§ TEACHING DEMONSTRATION

### **Preparation:**

**Typical Ask:** "30-minute lesson on [topic] for [Master's students/undergraduates]"

**Your Strategy:**
1. **Choose topic carefully** - Pick something you're expert in (NADES, SPME) or core to position (Green Chemistry Principles)
2. **Active learning required** - Don't just lecture! Include activity/discussion
3. **Start strong** - Hook them in first 2 minutes
4. **Time management** - Practice to finish in 25 min (leave time for Q&A)
5. **Prepare materials** - Handouts, slides, props

**Recommended Demonstration:**
- **Topic:** "Natural Deep Eutectic Solvents - Nature's Green Solvents"
- **Why:** Shows expertise, research integration, timely topic, engaging
- **Structure:**
  - Hook (2 min): "If I told you we could replace toxic solvents with sugar and salt, would you believe me?"
  - Overview (1 min): Learning objectives clear
  - Content (15 min): NADES principles, examples, properties
  - **ACTIVITY (8 min):** Groups design NADES for specific application, present
  - Wrap-up (4 min): Key takeaways, questions

**Bring:**
- Backup slides (USB + cloud)
- Handouts (enough for committee + 5 extras)
- Props if doing demo (actual NADES sample?)
- Whiteboard markers (don't assume they'll provide)

**During:**
- Make eye contact with committee
- Engage them (ask questions)
- Show enthusiasm (you love this!)
- Handle questions gracefully

## üìä RESEARCH PRESENTATION

### **Typical Format:**
- 45-60 minutes total (35-40 min talk + 10-20 min questions)
- Audience: Faculty (mixed chemistry backgrounds) + graduate students

### **Structure:**

**Slide 1: Title**
- Your name, current affiliation, topic

**Slides 2-3: Introduction (5 min)**
- Big picture: Why does this matter?
- Problem/gap
- Your approach

**Slides 4-15: Your Research (25-30 min)**
- PhD work (10 min) - Foundation
- Post-PhD/industry work (10 min) - Translation
- Current collaborations (5-10 min) - Malaysian connections

**Slides 16-20: Future Directions (5 min)**
- What you'll do at THEIR university
- Collaborations possible
- Student projects
- Funding strategy

**Slide 21: Acknowledgments**
- Collaborators, funding sources, thank audience

**Slide 22: Questions**

### **Tips:**
- **One message per slide** - Not text-heavy
- **Figures/data** - Show evidence, not just words
- **Tell story** - Not just "then I did this, then I did that"
- **Know your audience** - Define terms, explain context
- **Practice timing** - Absolutely do not run over
- **Anticipate questions** - Prepare answers to likely challenges

## üíº ONE-ON-ONE MEETINGS

### **With Faculty Members:**

**They want to know:**
- Can they work with you? (Collegial, collaborative)
- Will you strengthen department? (Research, teaching, service)
- Are you genuinely interested? (Not just any job)

**Your Approach:**
- **Do your homework:** Read their recent papers, know their research
- **Ask questions:** "I saw your paper on [X], fascinating approach to..."
- **Find connections:** "My SPME work could complement your metabolomics..."
- **Be genuine:** If no obvious connection, find common ground (teaching, mentoring, etc.)

### **With Department Head/Dean:**

**They assess:**
- Strategic fit with department direction
- Leadership potential
- Institutional commitment
- Budget implications (realistic about resources)

**Your Approach:**
- **Ask about vision:** "Where do you see the department in 5 years?"
- **Show how you fit:** "My green chemistry focus aligns with university sustainability goals..."
- **Be realistic:** "I understand startup package typically includes [X], I'd need [Y], can we discuss feasibility?"
- **Demonstrate leadership:** "I'm interested in curriculum development, potentially leading [initiative]..."

### **With Students:**

**They want:**
- Good mentor/supervisor
- Accessible, supportive
- Realistic about grad school

**Your Approach:**
- **Be honest:** "Grad school is challenging but rewarding..."
- **Describe your style:** "I meet weekly with students, hands-on initially, more independence as they develop..."
- **Ask them:** "What makes a good supervisor in your experience?"
- **Show interest:** "What are you working on?"

## üéØ QUESTIONS TO ASK THEM

### **For Search Committee:**
- "What are the department's strategic priorities for the next 5 years?"
- "What do you see as the biggest challenges for someone in this role?"
- "How does the department support early-career faculty?"
- "What's the typical path to promotion here?"

### **For Faculty:**
- "What do you enjoy most about working here?"
- "How would you describe the departmental culture?"
- "What opportunities exist for interdisciplinary collaboration?"
- "How involved are you in graduate student training?"

### **For Department Head:**
- "What resources are available for startup package?"
- "How does the department support grant writing?"
- "What's the teaching load expectation?"
- "Are there opportunities to develop new courses?"

### **For Students:**
- "What's the student culture like?"
- "How accessible are faculty supervisors typically?"
- "What career support does the program provide?"
- "What would you change about the program if you could?"

## üö´ RED FLAGS TO WATCH FOR

**During Visit:**
- Faculty seem unhappy or burnt out
- High turnover mentioned
- Vague about resources/support
- Unrealistic expectations ("Publish 10 papers/year while teaching 4 courses...")
- Toxic culture (gossip, complaints, divisions)
- Students seem stressed or unsupported

**If you see these:** Reconsider the position!
