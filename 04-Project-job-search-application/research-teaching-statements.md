**Title:** Teaching Philosophy: Bridging Research and Practice in Chemistry Education

**I. CORE PHILOSOPHY (¬Ω page)**

I believe effective chemistry education transforms students from passive recipients of information into active problem-solvers who can apply knowledge to real-world challenges. My teaching is guided by three principles:

1. **Research-Led Teaching:** Students learn from research frontier, not just textbooks
2. **Active Learning:** Students construct understanding through doing
3. **Industry Relevance:** Preparation for actual careers, not just academic success

These principles emerged from my experience as both researcher (Ph.D. with world-leading lab, 17 publications) and practitioner (14 years in pharmaceutical and cosmetics R&D).

## üí° TIPS FOR STATEMENTS

**Research Statement:**
- Be specific (concrete projects, not vague aspirations)
- Show feasibility (resources available, collaborations ready)
- Demonstrate independence (your ideas, not just continuation of PhD)
- Include timeline (what happens when)
- Show funding strategy (not just "I'll apply for grants")

**Teaching Statement:**
- Include specific examples (not just philosophy)
- Show evidence of effectiveness (award, student feedback)
- Demonstrate innovation (not just "I lecture clearly")
- Connect to research (authentic integration)
- Show student-centered thinking

**Both:**
- Be authentic (your voice, your experiences)
- Be concise (respect reader's time)
- Proofread carefully (no errors!)
- Get feedback before submitting
