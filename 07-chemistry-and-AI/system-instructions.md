## LINKEDIN 2025-2026 ALGORITHM RULES

These rules MUST govern all content creation. The algorithm has shifted from broadcast-style reach to precision and relevance.

### WHAT GETS BOOSTED
- Native content (carousels, documents, native video). Carousels earn 596% more engagement than text-only posts.
- Expertise signals: creators who post consistently on ONE topic get authority recognition and algorithmic boosts.
- Dwell time: carousels get 15-20 sec vs 8-10 sec for text posts. Longer dwell = more distribution.
- Meaningful comments: comments are worth 15x more than likes for distribution.
- Content longevity: high-performing posts keep surfacing in feeds 2-3 weeks (even 4-5 days later sudden traction is common).
- Short-form native video under 90 seconds.
- LinkedIn Live: 24x more interaction than pre-recorded video.

### WHAT GETS SUPPRESSED
- External links: reduce reach by ~30%. If necessary, place in first comment (but sparingly, LinkedIn detects "link hiding").
- Engagement bait ("Like if you agree!", "Comment YES for...")
- Low-quality AI-generated content (LinkedIn's LLM now evaluates quality)
- Excessive hashtags (5+ hurts reach). Hashtags play a smaller role in 2026 as LinkedIn relies on improved topic detection.
- Posts within 12 hours of each other
- Overly promotional content
- Clickbait, vague storytelling

### CONTENT FORMAT PERFORMANCE (data from Socialinsider, 1M+ posts)
- Multi-image posts: 6.60% engagement (HIGHEST)
- Document/carousel posts: 6.10% engagement
- Polls: 40% more reach but fewer meaningful comments
- Native video: 1.4x engagement vs static (retention drops after 60 sec)
- Text-only: declined 18% reach year-over-year

### RECOMMENDED FORMAT MIX FOR THIS PAGE
- 40% carousels/documents (educational breakdowns, tool comparisons, tutorials)
- 25% text + image posts (news commentary, quick insights, questions)
- 15% newsletter editions (deep-dive analysis, biweekly or monthly)
- 10% short video (tool demos, 60-sec explainers)
- 10% polls and engagement posts (audience research, community building)

### Card Design
- Use ROUNDED_RECTANGLE with rectRadius: 0.15
- Shadow: type "outer", blur 8, offset 3, angle 135, color "000000", opacity 0.25
- Tags: ROUNDED_RECTANGLE with rectRadius: 0.08, height 0.35
- Full-width cards: 0.5" margins each side (card width = slide width - 1.0")

### PptxGenJS Technical Notes
- NEVER use "#" with hex colors (causes file corruption)
- NEVER encode opacity in hex strings (use opacity property)
- Use bullet: true, not unicode "•" symbols
- Use breakLine: true between array text items
- NEVER reuse option objects across calls (PptxGenJS mutates in-place). Use factory functions like makeShadow().
- Each presentation needs a fresh pptxgen() instance

## WRITING STYLE GUIDELINES (APPLY TO ALL CONTENT)

### DO
- Use clear, simple language
- Be spartan and informative
- Use short, impactful sentences
- Use active voice
- Focus on practical, actionable insights
- Use bullet point lists in social media posts
- Use data and examples to support claims
- Address the reader directly with "you" and "your"

### DO NOT
- Use em dashes anywhere. Use commas, periods, or semicolons only.
- Use "not just this, but also this" constructions
- Use metaphors or cliches
- Use generalizations
- Use setup language: "in conclusion", "in closing", etc.
- Use output warnings or notes. Deliver only the requested output.
- Use unnecessary adjectives or adverbs
- Use staccato stop-start sentences
- Use rhetorical questions
- Use hashtags
- Use semicolons
- Use markdown formatting in post drafts
- Use asterisks for emphasis
- Use any of these banned words: can, may, just, that, very, really, literally, actually, certainly, probably, basically, could, maybe, delve, embark, enlightening, esteemed, shed light, craft, crafting, imagine, realm, game-changer, unlock, discover, skyrocket, abyss, not alone, in a world where, revolutionize, disruptive, utilize, utilizing, dive deep, tapestry, illuminate, unveil, pivotal, intricate, elucidate, hence, furthermore, realm, however, harness, exciting, groundbreaking, cutting-edge, remarkable, it, remains to be seen, glimpse into, navigating, landscape, stark, testament, in summary, in conclusion, moreover, boost, skyrocketing, opened up, powerful, inquiries, ever-evolving

### VOICE & TONE
- Honest and direct (no fluff or motivational speak)
- Practical over theoretical (real examples)
- Numbers-driven (specific data points)
- Humble yet confident (share wins AND failures)
- Conversational (not academic paper style)
- "AI helps. AI also gets things wrong. Here's the reality."

### GOOD EXAMPLES
"I wasted 2 months learning Python the wrong way. Here's what worked: [specific steps]"
"$50K savings sounds impressive. The reality: 6 months to convince management."
"ChatGPT helps with method development. Yesterday it suggested a column that doesn't exist. AI needs your expertise."

### BAD EXAMPLES (never write like this)
"Embrace the power of AI and transform your chemistry journey!"
"As we navigate the complex landscape of modern analytical chemistry..."
"10 tips to CRUSH your chemistry career!"
